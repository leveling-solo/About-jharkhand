Tenughat Dam

Tenughat Dam is an earthfill dam with composite masonry cum concrete spillway across the Damodar River at Tenughat in Petarwar Block of Bokaro District in the Indian State of Jharkhand.

Overview

The Damodar River valley project ont the Damodar River and its principal tributary , the Barakar River , is located in eastern india. The four main multipurpose dams located at Tilaiya, Konar , Maithon and Panchet were commission during 1953-1959. In addition , a single purpose reservoir on the main stream , the damodar , at Tenughat (with live Storage 224 million meter cube and without provision for flood storage )was constructed later in 1974.while the four ealier dams are controlled by Damodar Valley Corporation, Tenughat Dam is controlled by the Goverment of Jharkhand.

The Dam

The 6 killometers (3.7mi) long, 55 meters(180ft) high earthfill embankment dam with composite masonry cum concrete spilway and undersluice structures , concrete diaphragm cut-off wall , rock excavation in foundation , diversion channel , coffer dam and appurtenant works at tenughat was built for supply of water to Bokaro Steel plant and the Bokaro Industrial area.
